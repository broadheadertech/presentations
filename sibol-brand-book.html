i. narrative
— 01

The cinematic narrative.

A nation's first morning

SIBOL is not a soap. It is the first thing a country touches in the morning — and the last thing it trusts before sleep.

In every home — from the high-rise in BGC to the bamboo house in Bohol — there are two daily rituals that quietly equalize us.

A bar of soap on the skin. A scoop of detergent in the basin. Two small acts of dignity.

SIBOL begins in the soil. A coconut grower in Quezon. A calamansi farmer in Mindoro. A weaver of sampaguita garlands in Dangwa. A rice mother in Nueva Ecija. A beekeeper in Bukidnon. A lemongrass cooperative in Negros.

These are not suppliers. They are the first hands of the brand — co-authors of every bar. When SIBOL grows, they grow. Their daughters stay in school. Their sons stop migrating. Their barangay builds a roof that does not leak.

SIBOL is the Filipino household essential that the country has been waiting for: pang-masa but premium, modern but warm, profitable but principled. A soap that smells like home, looks like a gift, and carries the weight of a movement.

It is the simplest, most repeated acts in a Filipino day — washing one's hands, one's child, one's face, one's clothes — re-engineered into a daily vote for a fairer, cleaner, more hopeful Philippines.

This is not a product launch. This is the moment a country puts its hands on its own future.

ii. mark
— 02

The logo system.

A wordmark and a sprout

A custom lowercase wordmark, soft as a folded leaf — anchored by a single, characterful glyph: the SIBOL Sprout.

The lowercase decision is deliberate. Capital letters command. Lowercase invites. SIBOL must be felt, not declared. The "i" dot is replaced by a tiny rising shoot — the brand's secret signature, hidden at first, unforgettable once seen.

v. product
— 05

The product line.

Two ranges, one ritual of clean

Two ranges built from the same Filipino soil: The Bar Range — six all-natural bath soaps. The Wash Range — four plant-powered laundry detergents.

Naming follows a strict two-beat rhythm across both ranges: ingredient + benefit. Coconut Milk Care. Calamansi Fresh. Calamansi Lakas. Lemongrass Linis. Same brand DNA. Same shelf signal. Two categories that finally feel like they belong to the same Filipino home.

The Bar Range — soap.

Six all-natural bath soaps. 100g premium bar & 50g sachet bar.

Every bar variant ships in two formats: 100g premium bar for supermarkets & export · 50g paper-wrapped sachet for sari-sari accessibility.

The Wash Range — detergent.

Four plant-powered laundry detergents. Powder sachet, powder pouch & liquid bottle.

Every detergent variant ships in three formats: 900g pouch for the family · 70g powder sachet at ₱12 for sari-sari · 1L liquid bottle for the premium and export shelf.

vi. packaging
— 06

The packaging language.

Paper-first, plastic-last

Matte uncoated paper. Soybean ink. A subtle woven Filipino pattern pressed into the side seams. The whole bar feels closer to a gift box than a household soap — a daily moment of small luxury for every Filipino home.

i.

Recycled paper-board outer

FSC-certified uncoated paper. Compostable in 90 days. The wrapper itself can plant a seed — embedded basil or kamatis seeds in select editions.

ii.

Soy-ink printing

Vegetable-based inks. Zero glossy lamination. The matte hand-feel is a deliberate sensory signal: this is honest.

iii.

Embossed SIBOL seal

The monogram is debossed — not printed — into the front face. A tactile signature that survives the wettest hand and the longest journey.

iv.

Banig pattern accents

Subtle woven Filipino texture along the side seam. Quiet enough to feel premium. Filipino enough to be unmistakable.

viii. system
— 08

Product architecture.

Two pillars. One Filipino home.

SIBOL is a household cleaning brand built on two disciplined pillars: The Bar Range for the body and The Wash Range for the clothes.

The two ranges share a single soul — same name, same sprout, same six provinces of ingredients, same cooperative model. They sit side by side on every Filipino shelf: the bath section and the laundry section, both flying the same green flag. Ten core SKUs. No platform sprawl. No category drift. Just the two daily rituals that define a clean Filipino home — done together, finally, by one brand.

i.

One brand, two rituals.

The bar washes the body. The detergent washes the clothes. Same Filipino household, same trusted name, same green band across both shelves.

ii.

Shared ingredient soul.

Calamansi, lemongrass, sampaguita, niyog — these scents appear in both ranges. A SIBOL family smells like SIBOL from the bath to the laundry line.

iii.

Shared cooperative engine.

The same six provincial cooperatives feed both ranges. Calamansi farmers supply the bar and the detergent. One supply chain. Doubled livelihoods.

iv.

Disciplined depth.

Six bar variants. Four detergent variants. Three formats each. No seasonal noise, no line-extension creep. The discipline is what makes the brand uncopyable.

Closing manifesto
A nation does not rise all at once. It rises in small, daily, deliberate acts of care.

SIBOL is the smallest of those acts, repeated by a hundred million Filipino hands, every single morning — a quiet, fragrant, hopeful uprising. Linis. Lakas. Lago.
